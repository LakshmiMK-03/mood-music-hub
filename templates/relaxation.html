Relaxation & Stress Management

When medium or high stress levels are detected, the system provides breathing exercises, mindfulness suggestions, and break reminders to help manage stress effectively.

🌬 Breathing Exercise

Deep breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system, reducing cortisol and promoting calm.

Ready

Inhale 4 sec → Hold 4 sec → Exhale 4 sec

🧘 Mindfulness Suggestions

  • Acknowledge Your Feelings — Take a moment to recognize your emotions without judgment. Self-awareness is the first step toward emotional balance.
  • Stay Present — Focus on what you can control right now. Mindfulness reduces anxiety by anchoring you in the present moment.
  • Progressive Muscle Relaxation — Scan your body from head to toe and consciously relax tight muscles to release physical tension.
  • Guided Visualization — Close your eyes and imagine a peaceful scene — a quiet beach, a green forest, or a calm sunset — to activate relaxation responses.

⏰ Break Reminders

  • 20-20-20 Rule — Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds to reduce eye strain.
  • Stretch Break — Stand up every 30–45 minutes and stretch for 2–3 minutes. Movement increases blood flow and reduces fatigue.
  • Hydration Check — Drink water regularly. Dehydration can increase cortisol (stress hormone) levels.
